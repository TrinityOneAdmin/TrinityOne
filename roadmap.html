Keykeeper — keep your church key on a device of its own

Today a steward’s key lives on their everyday phone or laptop, encrypted behind a PIN — which protects against a lost or borrowed device. Keykeeper goes a step further: it holds the key on a separate signer (a small hardware device, or an isolated extension), so the everyday app only ever gets signatures, never the secret.

  • ✓A hacked computer can’t post in your church’s name or steal the key
  • ✓Approve sensitive actions with a physical button (hardware version)
  • ✓Same recovery phrase — restore on any device, or the app itself
⏳
Honest take: for a small church the PIN lock already covers the everyday risks. Keykeeper is for when the stakes rise — real giving, larger congregations, or the security-conscious. A nice-to-have, not a day-one essential.

Modules & add-ons

TrinityOne's core — reading, church chat, groups, serving rotas, the calendar and teaching — is free forever. On top of that, a church switches on just the modules it needs. Most are free; a few are optional add-on services (the ones that cost real money to run — livestreaming, video calls) that help keep the project going. Never a paywall on fellowship. Most of these are still in build — here's the honest slate.
