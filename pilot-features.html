TrinityOne

Features for our pilot church

A plain-language walk through everything TrinityOne can do — the Bible, the way we gather, serve, care for one another, and keep our people safe. Here's what you get from day one, and what's still on the way.

In the pilot Live now — ready to use from day one. Coming later On the way after the pilot settles.

Bible & study

God's Word is the heart of it all — everything else here simply draws us back to time in Scripture together.

The Bible reader In the pilot

A full Bible the moment you open the app, in clean, book-quality type — and it works completely offline once it's on your phone. Tap a word to see its original-language root, and highlight verses like a real marker.

Downloadable translations In the pilot

Thousands of translations to download in your own language, so everyone can read in the words they know best. You only download what you want to keep.

The app ships without any Bibles built in and fetches them on demand — so the download stays small and works over a thin connection.

Reading plans In the pilot

A little to read each day, guided — follow the church's plan or one of the built-in ones, day by day.

The Library In the pilot

Your own shelf of everything you've marked in the Word: highlights, bookmarks, and personal notes — plus cross-references and commentaries to dig deeper, and fast search across the Bibles you've installed.

A cross-reference points you to other verses on the same theme; a commentary is a trusted writer's notes on a passage.

Every day

A gentle home screen that gives you something for today — and keeps you in the Word without having to go looking.

Today In the pilot

Your church's day at a glance: where you are in your reading, what's coming up, and anything that needs you — all on one warm opening screen.

Verse of the day In the pilot

A single verse to sit with each morning, right where you'll see it.

Devotionals In the pilot

Short daily readings the church can write and publish to everyone — a thought to carry through the day, tied back to a passage.

Community

The conversations that keep a church close through the week — carrying one another between Sundays.

Group chats In the pilot

Rooms for life groups, ministries and prayer — share news, send a prayer, or post a verse with your own thoughts. There's a whole-church room for announcements everyone sees.

Invite-only groups In the pilot

Private rooms for what's meant to stay close — a leaders' group, a small accountability circle — where only invited people can read and take part.

Direct messages In the pilot

Message anyone in the church one-to-one, privately and encrypted, so only the two of you can read it.

"Encrypted" means the words are scrambled to everyone but you and the person you're writing to — not even the server can read them.

People directory In the pilot

See who's in your church, find a person and start a message. Everyone shows up with the name and photo they've set.

Teams In the pilot

Serving teams (welcome, worship, kids, coffee) each have their own place — so the people who serve together can talk together.

Serving & events

The whole church's week in one place, so nothing slips by and everyone knows when it's their turn.

Serving rotas In the pilot

See when you're next on, accept your slot or ask a teammate to swap, and set the days you're away — with a gentle reminder that reaches you even with the app closed.

Run sheets In the pilot

The order of a service or event, step by step, so everyone serving knows what happens when.

Calendar & events In the pilot

Every service, group, event and your own rota on one warm calendar. Tap to say you're going.

Room booking In the pilot

See which rooms are free and book a space for a meeting or group, so two things don't land in the same room at once.

For stewards: you build the rota, calendar and rooms once from the console, and the app handles the asking, swapping and reminders for everyone.

Caring for one another

When someone needs the church to show up — a new baby, a hard week, someone home from hospital — this makes it easy to rally round, so no one is forgotten.

Care needs In the pilot

The church opens a need — meals, rides, errands, visits or childcare — and members sign up to help, day by day.

Meal trains In the pilot

Fill the days one at a time: a meal Tuesday, a ride Thursday, errands Saturday. The person receiving help can mark the days they don't need anything, so no one turns up unwanted.

"I'll help" In the pilot

One tap to sign up for a day — "I'll bring Tuesday dinner." A quiet care team keeps an eye on it all and sends reminders.

"I'm here to help" In the pilot

Tell your church you're glad to lend a hand — tick what you can help with (meals, lifts, visits, prayer, childcare, errands) and add a note like "weekday evenings are easiest for me." Anyone going through a hard time then sees a warm "Ready to help" list and can message you directly, or ask the care team.

It flips the burden: instead of someone having to ask, the offer is already there — making it that much easier to reach out.

Giving & finance

Handling the church's provision honestly and privately — with the money-in and money-out pieces designed to arrive together, once they're properly reviewed.

Finance module (for the treasurer) In the pilot

An optional treasurer's ledger — record giving, manage funds and donors, and (where it applies) build a UK Gift Aid claim. Off unless you turn it on, and locked to the church's own key so only the church can read it.

Optional in the pilot — a church that doesn't need it simply leaves it switched off.

Member giving from your own wallet Coming later

Down the road, members will be able to give tithes and offerings straight from their own wallet — the church receives it directly, with no card processor taking a cut and no in-app balance to top up.

Manna — giving-boxes for those in need Coming later

A way for the church to give out from its own fund to people in need — with a "coming alongside" flow of vouching and care, not just a transfer. Being designed to land alongside member giving.

Links with local care charities Coming later

A future way to connect trusted local charities so their practical needs — drivers, befrienders, transport — reach your church's willing helpers.

Keeping people safe

The young people in our care are guarded by the same trust we extend in person — anchored in our own leaders, not a faraway company.

Child accounts & cleared adults In the pilot

Your leaders mark who is a minor and who is an approved, cleared adult — using your own country's background-check process. Nothing is tied to one nation's scheme.

Guardians In the pilot

Young people are linked to a parent or guardian, and messages between an adult and a minor pass through that guardian — so a child is never in a private thread unwatched.

Join approval In the pilot

New people asking to join don't just walk in — a steward approves each one, so the church stays exactly who you know it to be.

For stewards: the safeguarding lists are leader-only, and your existing safeguarding policy fits around this rather than being replaced by it.

For stewards

Running the church behind the scenes — one simple console where a leader sets everything up, and the app carries it to everyone.

The steward console In the pilot

Your control room in a web page: an overview of the church, and tabs for groups, the rota, the calendar, rooms, resources (plans and devotionals) and members. Set things up once and they appear in everyone's app.

A "steward" is simply a church leader with the keys to set things up — the word we use instead of "admin."

Delegated stewards In the pilot

Share the load safely. A leader invites another person, who requests access and is approved with a PIN. Each gets their own key, and access can be taken back at any time — no one has to hand around a single master password.

The Finance module In the pilot

The treasurer's optional ledger (see Giving & finance) — switched on only if your church needs it.

Care management In the pilot

Open care needs, see who's signed up for what, send reminders and mark them done. A real care team — not just one person — can run it together.

Security & console lock In the pilot

The console can be locked behind a PIN, so a borrowed or lost device can't be used to post in the church's name.

Backup In the pilot

Save a sealed, encrypted backup of the church's data and keys, so nothing is lost if a device is. Only you can unlock it with your passphrase.

Private church directory / phone book In the pilot

Like a paper church directory, but private and encrypted — the steward types in names and phone numbers and every accepted member gets a read-only copy. For the hospital call, the check-in, the member who doesn't watch chat.

First-launch onboarding wizard In the pilot

When a member first opens the app, a friendly wizard walks them through setting up and following your church — no forms, nothing to remember.

Owning it

TrinityOne is built so a church truly owns its own home — kept private, held on ground you control, and free forever.

Church networks In the pilot

Churches that belong together — a movement, a denomination, a group of plants — can link up, so shared resources and news can reach across them while each church stays its own.

Your own identity In the pilot

No email, no password, no company holding your account — just a name your church knows, backed by your own private key. Your profile name and photo travel with you to any device.

That key is called an "npub" — think of it as your own signature. Written down once as a short recovery phrase, it gets you back in anywhere. No one, not us and not your church, can ever ask you for it.

Self-hostable relay In the pilot

A church can run all of this on its own small computer, behind its own domain — so your messages and data live on your hardware, not a company's.

A "relay" is just the private little server that holds your church's messages. Host your own for full independence, or let us host it to get started.

Open protocols (Nostr) In the pilot

TrinityOne is built on open, shared standards rather than a company's locked box — so no single business can switch it off, hold your church's data hostage, or lock you in.

"Nostr" is the open messaging standard underneath — the same reason your email works between any providers, applied to your church.

Privacy & resilience for churches under pressure In the pilot

Built first for the persecuted church: your messages are private to your church, work over the thinnest connection, and stay yours even where it isn't safe to be online. What keeps them safe protects everyone else too.

Always free & open source In the pilot

The heart of TrinityOne is free forever, and the code is open for anyone to read and check. Never a paywall on fellowship.

Gathering when the internet is down Coming later

A future way to keep the church talking even with no internet — a small box on your building's own Wi-Fi so the gathering keeps working through an outage. On the way, not in the pilot yet.

A few things are still on the way — member giving from your own wallet, Manna (giving to those in need), links with local care charities, and gathering when the internet is down. We'd rather get the heart of it right first, and add the rest in time.
