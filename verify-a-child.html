How a child is verified

TrinityOne keeps young people safe by anchoring trust in your church — the people who actually know who the children are — not a meaningless "are you over 18?" tick-box. This is how a child gets verified, and what changes once they are.

★
The principle. A child is only ever marked by a steward (or confirmed from a parent's request) — never self-declared. This works alongside your church's safeguarding (background-checked leaders, policy), and never replaces it.

What "verified" unlocks

Once a member is verified as a child, the relay (the server carrying your church's messages) enforces two protections automatically — they can't be switched off by tampering with a phone:

  • ✓
    Child-safe groups only. A verified child sees and joins only the groups you've marked child-safe. Adult spaces never appear for them.
  • ✓
    Protected private messages. A one-to-one message between a child and an adult is blocked unless that adult is cleared for youth (or is the child's own linked parent). Both directions.
  • ✓
    Your leaders stay reachable. A child can always message the church itself if they need help.

Two ways to verify a child

Most churches use the first; the second is for parents setting up a younger child.

Method 1 — A steward marks them recommended

If a young person is already in your church, a steward marks them directly.

1
Open the steward console → Members. Find the young person in the list.
2
Tap “Child”. A clay child badge appears. That's it — they're verified, and the two protections above kick in.

Method 2 — A parent sets up the child, you confirm

A parent can create and hold their child's account from their own phone, then ask you to confirm the link. This is the best route for younger children.

1
The parent adds the child in their app: You → My family → Children's accounts → Add a child. They keep the child's recovery words and hand the account to the child's device with a one-scan code.
2
You get a request to confirm. In the steward console's Members tab, a “Parent / child links to confirm” card appears.

Make your groups child-safe

So a verified child has somewhere to be, mark the groups they belong in as child-safe: in Groups, use the Child-safe toggle on each group (e.g. Youth, Whole-church notices). Groups left un-marked stay hidden from children.

⚠️
This supports your safeguarding — it doesn't replace it. It doesn't perform background checks, supervise relationships, or read message content; it applies your decisions. Keep your policy, training and supervision fully in place, and speak to your safeguarding lead with any concern.
