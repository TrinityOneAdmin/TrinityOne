Features, in depth

Everything a church needs — nothing it doesn't own

The short version is on the home page. This is the long one: every part of TrinityOne, what it does, and why it works the way it does.

Read

A Bible you'll want to stay in

A complete offline Bible the moment you open the app, set in clean, book-quality type — warm paper by day, gentle dark mode at night. Tap any word for its Greek or Hebrew root, and highlight like a real marker.

  • Berean Standard installed on first launch — works fully offline
  • 1,000+ translations to download, from a live eBible.org mirror
  • Strong's word study, cross-references & commentaries
  • Highlights, notes & bookmarks — kept on your device, never harvested

Gather

Real conversation, no social-media baggage

Chat runs on Nostr — an open protocol — so there's no account to create and nothing to mine. Life-group, ministry and prayer rooms for your church, and a wider network to gather across congregations.

  • Life-group, ministry & prayer rooms
  • Share verses & say “Amen” to prayer requests
  • End-to-end encrypted direct messages
  • Invite-only and fully-encrypted groups
  • No account — no email or phone number

Serving

Know when you're on — and swap in a tap

The serving rota lives in your pocket. See when you're next on, who's on the team beside you, and the time to arrive. Can't make it? Ask a teammate to swap, or mark yourself away — no awkward group texts.

  • Accept or decline a serving request
  • Ask someone to swap — nothing changes till they say yes
  • A push notification when you're asked — even with the app closed
  • Set the days you're away so you're not asked
For stewards: build the rota from the console, with run-sheets for each role and team, and the app handles the asking, swapping and reminders.

Safeguarding

Protection anchored in your leaders

The young people in your care are guarded by the same trust you already extend in person — not a meaningless “are you 18?” tick-box. Your leaders set who is a minor and who is an approved, checked adult, and the app enforces it everywhere.

  • Child-safe groups — adults present, conversations visible to leaders
  • Direct messages between a child and an adult are gated
  • Parents linked to their children, in control
  • Enforced on the relay — a tampered app can't bypass it
For stewards: the safeguarding lists are owner-only, and each country's own background-check process fits — nothing is hard-wired to one nation's scheme.

Giving & Treasury

Giving that lands with the church — not a processor

  • Encrypted donor records & fund categories

Self-hosting

Run the whole thing on a box you own

A church can run its own relay on a Raspberry Pi, mini-PC or VPS — one line in a terminal, then finish in the browser. Your messages and data live on your hardware, behind your own domain. No company in the middle, nothing to switch off.

  • One-line installer — sets up the relay as a boot service
  • A secure public address via Cloudflare — or your own domain
  • Signed self-updates — the relay keeps itself current, safely
  • Walk away anytime with all of your church's data

Your keys & privacy

No passwords. No company holding your data. Just you.

TrinityOne has no account and no password — your device holds a private key that is you. That's wonderful for privacy, and it means one gentle, one-time step keeps your identity yours: write down twelve words.

  • No email, no phone number — just a name your church knows
  • Twelve recovery words — get back in on any device
  • Strict CSP & an optional PIN protect the key on your device
  • Study notes & highlights stay on your device, never harvested
