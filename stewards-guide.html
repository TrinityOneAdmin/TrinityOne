⛪ Stewards

Letting others help run a church

There are two ways to share control of a church. They look similar but work very differently — and the difference is mostly about whether you can undo it.

The two ways, at a glance

Delegated steward (recommended)Full handoff
What you shareNothing secret — you send an invite, they request, you approve. The church key is never sharedThe church’s recovery phrase (the master key)
They becomeA helper — post, manage who joins, create groups & studies. They act under their own keyA co-owner — full control, same as you
Undo it?Yes, instantly — revoke them and they lose accessNo — once they have the phrase, they have it for good (rotate the church to undo)
Use it forVolunteers, a second leader you might changeSomeone you trust to be the church forever
★
Rule of thumb: share the recovery phrase only with someone you’d trust to be the church permanently. For everyone else, make them a delegated steward.

Which app does each person need?

Everyone here uses the Steward app (the console) — not the member app. The member app is for taking part as a member; only the Steward app can act on a church’s behalf.

  • ●
    The owner runs the Steward app holding the church key.
  • ●
    A delegated steward runs the Steward app with their own key (the app makes one when they start the steps below). The church key is never copied to their device.
  • ●
    A full-handoff co-owner runs the Steward app and restores the church from its recovery phrase.

Make someone a delegated steward

This is the revocable way, and nothing secret changes hands. It’s a three-step handshake — the owner invites, the helper requests, the owner approves.

1
The owner · Steward app

Send an invite

Go to Settings → Security → Delegated stewards → Add a steward. It shows an invite — a QR to scan, or Copy invite to send as text.

2
The helper · Steward app

Open the invite & request

Open the Steward app and choose Settings → Security → Become a steward (or, on the very first run, the Help run a church option in the wizard) → Scan a church’s invite (or paste it — there’s a no-camera paste fallback). This sends a request to the owner. The app makes your own key and offers a PIN to protect it — set one.

3
The owner · Steward app

Approve the request

You’ll see “someone wants to help steward” — a banner on the Overview and under Delegated stewards. Tap to approve (PIN-gated if you’ve set a console PIN). Check the friendly name shown matches the person you meant to add.

4
The helper

Switch to the church

Within a few seconds, the church appears in their top-left switcher as “acting as steward of .” Tap it (a STEWARD badge shows) and they can post announcements, manage who joins, and create groups & studies.

★
The friendly name is your cross-check. Every identity has an automatically-generated name (e.g. “Quiet Olive 28”). It shows on both sides, so when the owner approves a request they can confirm the name matches the person they meant to add.
↩︎
To remove a steward (instant): the owner goes to Settings → Security → Delegated stewards and taps Revoke. It takes effect immediately for new actions — they can no longer act for the church. (Their past announcements may stay visible; they just can’t post new ones.)

Hand a church over completely (full handoff)

1
The owner · Steward app

Show the handoff QR

Settings → Security → Stewards & handoff → Show handoff QR (or reveal the 12-word phrase). This carries the master key — show it in person, only to someone you fully trust.

2
The other person · Steward app

Restore the church

On the first screen tap Restore a church → Scan the QR (or paste the phrase). They now hold the church too. Handing it over entirely? The original holder can then Remove it from this device.

Protecting the key with a PIN

A church key — and a steward key — can act for a church, so keep it locked. Set or change a PIN under Settings → Security → Console lock; it encrypts the key on the device and asks for the PIN to open the console. Removing the PIN requires entering it, so a left-open console can’t be stripped. Don’t forget it — without the PIN (or the recovery phrase) that device can’t open the church.

What a delegated steward can and can’t do

Can

  • ✓Post announcements & broadcasts
  • ✓Manage who joins (approve / admit)
  • ✓Create content — groups & studies

Can’t (owner only)

  • ✕Add or remove other stewards
  • ✕Ban members or change the church key
  • ✕Change the relay / network settings

If it’s “not showing up”

  • ●
    Owner didn’t see the request? Look for the “someone wants to help steward” banner on the Overview and under Delegated stewards — the helper must have sent the request (step 2) first.
  • ●
    Steward sees no “acting as steward of …”? Make sure the owner has approved the request, then give it a few seconds to find the roster.
  • ●
    On an old build? Reinstall the latest APK, or refresh the web console (the version is at the foot of Settings).
