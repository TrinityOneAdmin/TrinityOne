The path, step by step

1

Start with one group

Don't move the whole church on day one. Set TrinityOne up, create a single room — your leadership team, or one life group — and live in it for a week or two. Get a feel for the chat, events and serving.

And you don't have to switch the whole system on at once. Most of TrinityOne — the Library, Finance, kids' check-in and more — turns on and off under Settings → Congregation features. Begin with just chat and a Bible, and add the rest only as you're ready.

Why: a small win builds confidence (yours and theirs) and surfaces any "how do I…" questions while the stakes are low.
2

Re-create your groups

In the Steward console → Groups, make a room for each group you want to bring over. Choose open for anything church-wide (everyone sees and joins it), or invite-only for a closed group like a specific small group or your leadership. It's a five-minute job — and you get child-safe controls, an encrypted option, broadcast channels and polls that WhatsApp can't do.

A WhatsApp group is really just a name and a set of people. You re-make the name here; the people arrive in step 3.
3

Bring your people across

Use both routes — they cover everyone:

Drop your join link into each WhatsApp / Facebook group — "We're moving to TrinityOne, tap to join 👉". It shows a tidy preview card and opens TrinityOne instantly in their browser, already following the church and showing their name. No app to download, no account, no password.

For anyone not in those chats, show or print the church's invite poster — one QR the whole room can scan — or just text them the same join link. (Your joining code has Copy link, Share and Save QR right there in the console.)

Every route lands people on the same one-scan page. For an open group they're in instantly; for an invite-only group you add them with a tap once they appear in Members.
4

Move your records

Your giving records import straight from the CSV your old church software exports — Finance → import. Your directory fills itself in, because everyone arrives already named from step 3. Rotas and the calendar take a few minutes to re-create.

Records stay encrypted to your church key — they live with you, not on someone else's server.
5

Go live, gently

Once people are in and comfortable, make TrinityOne the place for the day-to-day. Keep the old tool around read-only for a season if it helps people settle — there's no rush, and no moment where everything has to switch at once.

What comes over — and what doesn't

✓ Comes with you

  • Your groups (re-created, with more control)
  • Your people — by their real names
  • Giving records & statements
  • Rotas, events and rooms

✕ Stays behind

  • Phone numbers (TrinityOne needs none)
  • Old chat history & forwards
  • Monthly fees
  • A company sitting in the middle

Leaving the history behind isn't a loss — it's a clean, quiet start. The relationships come; the noise doesn't.

Your data is yours — really

TrinityOne is self-custodial: your church is a key you hold, not an account a company holds. No one can lock you out, raise your rent, or sell your data — and if you ever decided to leave, you'd take everything with you. That's the whole point, and it's why trying it costs you nothing but a little time.

Free — and it stays free

Not free-for-now. There's no subscription that starts after setup, no per-member fee, no "pro" tier, no limits to unlock, no trial clock, and no card to enter — ever. TrinityOne can be free because there's no company in the middle hosting it for you, so there's nothing for anyone to bill you for. Move your whole church in, grow to any size, and the price stays exactly the same: nothing.
