Safeguarding

Watch over the little ones.

Your church knows its people — so protection is anchored in your leaders, not a meaningless “are you 18?” tick-box. The young people in your care are guarded by the same trust you already extend in person.

No stranger can reach a child

A young person can only be messaged by an adult your leaders have cleared to work with youth — in both directions. It’s enforced for real, not a setting a curious child can flip.

Parents stay close to their own

A parent and their child are linked, so mum and dad can always reach their own children — and you can see, at a glance, that every young person has a guardian who’s watching.

Anchored in who you trust

Your steward marks who’s a child and which adults are cleared — mirroring decisions you’ve already made in real life. Nothing is shown as a public badge; it quietly shapes what each account can do.

These tools support the care you already take — your policy, your background-checked leaders, your supervision. They’re the digital side lining up with the real one; they never replace it. How a child is verified →

For pastors & stewards

Bring your whole church on — in an afternoon

No servers to buy, no accounts to manage. Your church gets a key, you publish your funds and groups, and your people join with a code. You own all of it.

  1. 1

    Open the Steward console

    Stewards run the church from the Steward console — in your browser, or as an app. It mints your church’s identity — an npub — and holds the key that signs every group and notice, locked on the device with a PIN.

  2. 2

    Set up your groups

    Create your chat groups — whole-church announcements, life groups, prayer, youth — broadcast, open, or invite-only. Each publishes as a signed event your members can find.

  3. 3

    Choose your relays

    Start instantly on TrinityOne’s shared relays — zero setup — or add your own from the Steward console for full control. Publish to a few so everything loads fast and stays yours.

  4. 4

    Share the joining code

    Hand members a short code or QR at the welcome desk. They scan, follow, and they’re in — no email or phone number needed.
