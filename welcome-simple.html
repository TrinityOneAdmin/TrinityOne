How it works

Up and running in three simple steps

No technical know-how needed. If you can use a phone, you can use TrinityOne.

  1. 1

    A leader sets it up

    A church leader gets the church started. It's free, and it takes about an afternoon.

  2. 2

    Choose where it lives

    Let us host it for you, or host your own — whichever suits.

    A relay is just the private server that holds your church's messages. Most churches let us host it.

  3. 3

    Everyone joins

    Your people join by tapping a link. No forms to fill in, nothing to remember.

Made for every church

One app, for the whole family of God

From believers gathering quietly where it isn't safe to be online, to a church with a full Sunday rota and online giving. The same app, kept private to your church.

Where it isn't safe

For the church under pressure, this is a quiet, private place to gather. Nothing is held by a big company that could be forced to hand it over or switch it off. It works over the thinnest connection, and a church can hold it on its own server if it has to.

A full week's life

For the well-resourced church, it's the whole week in one place: the Bible, groups, prayer, this week's teaching, serving rotas, watching over the children — and giving, for churches set up to receive it.

Keeping things private to your church is how we keep churches under pressure safe — and it protects everyone else along the way.
